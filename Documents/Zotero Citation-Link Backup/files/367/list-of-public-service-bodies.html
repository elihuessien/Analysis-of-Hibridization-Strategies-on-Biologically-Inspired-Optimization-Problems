    • List of Public Service Bodies

    Section 5(5) of the Regulation of Lobbying Act 2015 (the Act) provides for a number of "excepted communications" (exempt communications) which are not regarded as lobbying.  The exemptions at sections 5(5)(e), 5(5)(k), 5(5)(l) and 5(5)(n) of the Act make reference to a "public service body".  Section 7 of the Act sets out a definition of a “public service body” for the purposes of the Act.

    The exemption at section 5(5)(k) of the Act provides that communications made by a person who is employed by or holds any office or other position in a public service body or is engaged by a public service body are exempt provided the communications are made in that capacity and the communications relate to the functions of the public service body.

    Below is a list of bodies which have been confirmed as public service bodies for the purposes of the Act.  This list is not exhaustive and may change. If a person is unsure as to whether an organisation is considered a public service body for the purposes of the Act, they should contact the Standards Commission for further information.
